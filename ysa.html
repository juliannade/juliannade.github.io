Youth Spirit Artworks: Guide to a Tiny Home Village

A guide book for building supportive tiny housing

Background

Youth Spirit Artworks (YSA) is an interfaith art jobs and job training program located which is committed to empowering homeless and low-income San Francisco Bay Area young people. In January of 2016, youth at YSA initiated a community organizing campaign to build a Tiny House Village for homeless youth in the San Francisco East Bay Area. As of 2021, they've built 26 beautiful, youth-designed houses with the help of over 2,000 volunteers

In 2018, for my final porject for my sustainable design course at UC BerkeleyI worked with a team to research, write an design a book about Youth Spirit Artworks, and their campaign to build tiny homes for homeless youth.

We had a month to conceptualize, write, design, and print our book. We based the style on the How-To genre, and split the book into 3 different sections. The first section focused on policies, funding and community outreach. The second section focused on site design and programing. The third section focused on design of the tiny home unit.

Research

I conducted research and wrote the first section which focused on policies, funding and community outreach. Using Youth Spirit Artworks as a case study I researched which policies would help fast-track the permitting process, as well as, different funding avenues and the requirements to get funding, and the effects of supportive housing on the neighborhoods that they inhabit.

Writing

I split the first section into 3 sections, which answered 3 questions:
1. How to secure a site?
2. How to secure funding?
3. How to gain community support?

The first section described using inforgraphic the steps in order to secure a site for supportive hosuing. The second I descibed different avenues available for funding. The thrid section I wrote about the importance of community education, as NIMBYism is a large issue when it come to supportive housing. The book also included an op-ed I wrote for the East BAy Times, describing my experience witha supportive hosuing communittiy in my neighborhood.

Design

I helped develop the overall design scheme in our book. We used the color scheme that the organization Youth Spirit Artworks already used in their branding. Our team came up with the idea to use houses in our deisgns and then I created the inforgraphics used throuout the book on that premise, as well as the graphics on the front and back cover.
