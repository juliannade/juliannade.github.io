Santa Clara Towers

An elegant landing page

Details

Background

Santa Clara Towers are a pair of prominent office towers near Highway 101 in Santa Clara. The client, Hines, recently purchased the buildings and looked to the marketing team at Cushmman and Wakefeild to market the new leasing avialabilities.

Our San Francisco team was tasked with repositioning the project which included a rebrand, print brochure, email campaign, and marketing website. I worked with the print designer to incorporate the print design into a splash page to quickly inform web visitors about the buidlings.

I worked on...

Design

The first challenge was incorporating the print design and desired content within the limited space of a splash page. To increase visability of the background image, and utilise design language from the brochure, I used a diagonal menu which shared the same shape as the brochure as the graphics from the brochure cover.

Development

The challenge of deveopment was maintaining the same appearnce on all screen sizes. The background image, diagonal menu and location buttons would shift depending on the screen size, so careful attenton had to be paid. I used the Z-index property and a cutout of the backround image, so the lines would always appear to come from behind the builings.

My Process

Siteplan

Worked with team and client to determine all copy and information needed for splash page

Wireframe

Created a basic design outline that could fit all copy and content for multiple screen sizes

Mock Up

Created high fidelity mock up in the Sketch application which included design elements from the brochure, inlcuding the triangular elelemnt for the menu.

Development

Using Bootstrap for styling, special attention was paid to the tower circle buttons, and the lines anchoring them behind the buildings.

The Outcome

The result is a concise and elegant website that is consistent with the brochure design.
