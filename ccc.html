About

Century City Center

A multipage luxury real estate website

Details

Background

Century City Center is a 37-story tower in development in Los Angeles which provides approximately 700,000 square feet of corporate office space with supporting retail, dining and other amenities. It will be distinguishable by its tapered and highly sculptured profile, it deserves a debut worthy of its presence and standing as a new and iconic mark on the Los Angeles skyline.

Our San Francisco marketing team at Cushman & Wakefield was tasked with the marketing campaign which included a brand development, print brochure, email campaign, and marketing website. I was brought onto the project to design and develop the website.

I worked on...

Design

The design process went through a few iterations to discern the creative direction of the site. The main challenge was incorporating the style of the brochure while maintaining a digital feel. The final approved design was very imagery focused to display the beautiful renderings of the building and its amenities.

Development

My main focus during development was ensuring a smooth and seamless experience for users while including aspects that can take longer to load, such as full screen imagery and virtual tours. I implemented lazy loading in order to speed up loading times.

My Process

Site Plan

I worked with the clients to build a list of all content, copy, and imagery that they wanted included in the site.

Wireframe

I created wireframes/rough sketches for each page to get an idea of the content placement and look/feel of the site, in order to develop them into high fidelity mockups for client approval.

Mockup

I went through a few rounds of high fidelity mockups for the client, making changes each time until each page of the site was approved.

Development

I developed the site in HTML using the Bootstrap framework for styling, and utilized javascript libraries for the lazy loading of images and the virtual tour hosting.

The Outcome

The result is a polished design that incorporates aspects of the print collateral, while remaining distinctly digital. These are just a few selected pages, to view the full site please visit here.

Homepage
